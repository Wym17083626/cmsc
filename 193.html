Western Conservation Finance Boot Camp at Stanford University

Recognizing the need for new training vehicles for conservation finance techniques, Island Press and The Woods Institute for the Environment at Stanford University hosted and sponsored the first Western Conservation Finance Boot Camp from February 1–5, 2010. Other sponsors include the Land Trust Alliance, the Trust for Public Land, and the Conservation Finance Forum. 

Story Clark of Conservation Consulting created the curriculum and recruited the speakers for the camp. The Boot Camp offered training at the intermediate to advanced level on innovative and underutilized conservation finance techniques of particular value to western land conservationists. Although the Boot Camp structure was intense and fast-paced, it was also informal and congenial, capped at a modest number of participants so that attendees could comfortably interact with and learn from both national experts and each other.

Twenty-five conservationists attended the five-day Boot Camp. An additional eight conservationists joined for the first two days. In total, thirty-three attendees came to Stanford from fourteen states (WY, OR, KS, CO, CA, TX, ID, MA, VA, AZ, WA, HI, DC, MI). Because of the camp’s location and the high concentration of land conservation organizations in California, this state was best represented with nine participants. The regional focus also attracted the leaders from western agricultural land trusts, representing over one third of the Camp attendees.

The Stanford Boot Camp focused on western, primarily rural, conservation finance techniques, both to compliment the audience’s interests, and also to avoid replication of the previous conservation camps held at Yale University, which have a national focus.  The success of the Boot Camp at Stanford University is a strong indicator of the usefulness and importance of the finance camp program, which was first held at Yale in 2007.

For more information about the Stanford Camp, please contact Kate Graves at kgraves@islandpress.org.
