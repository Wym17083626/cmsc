Persian Poetry Offers View Into Medieval Islamic Culture

Shahzad Bashir, an associate professor of religious studies. has recently begun a research project investigating the idea of the past in medieval Persian poetry, exploring the immense troves of poetry and prose produced during the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries. He finds that love is one of the most pervasive themes, used by poets to reflect societal values.

Exploring the Popularization of the Mixed Race American

Are there proportionally more mixed race Americans today then twenty or a hundred years ago? Or has something changed about how Americans see mixed race?

Stanford scholar Michele Elam investigates these questions in her latest research for The Souls of Mixed Folk: Race, Politics, and Aesthetics in the New Millennium.
